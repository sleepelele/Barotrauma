⚠ EuropaCorp Crew Recruitment Notice
REF: EURO-SUB-CREW-7 — ALL POSITIONS OPEN — READ CAREFULLY OR DON'T, HONESTLY I DON'T CARE, AS LONG AS YOU SIGN AT THE BOTTOM.
On the 4th of October, 2197, the submarine Dugong was declared lost with all hands at a depth of 4,000 meters somewhere in Sector 9 of the Europa Deep. The hull did not so much fail as it ceased to have opinions about pressure. The implosion was, by all accounts, instantaneous — so we hope. The paperwork was significantly slower. EuropaCorp has closed the investigation. The investigation found nothing. This is because there is nothing left to find. We remember the crew as follows: SLEEP — Chief Medical Officer. Unlicensed. Largely self-taught, primarily on others. Sleep approached medicine with the enthusiasm of a man who genuinely enjoyed the process more than the outcome. His drug cabinet was famously communal, his bedside manner was optional, and his research ethics were the subject of at least three internal complaints, all of which he personally signed off on. Patients reported waking up from routine procedures missing things they were reasonably attached to. Sleep reported this as "successful surplus extraction." He tended to keep some extra organs in storage just in case (mostly dealing with the black market as a side hustle). BADLANDER — Captain. A man of few words and many fists, Badlander commanded the Dugong with an iron grip, a pipe of questionable tobacco, and a personal philosophy that discipline begins with the electrician. His relationship with Zez was described by crew members as "complicated," by Zez as "please stop," and by Badlander as "character building." The ballast compartment in Section 2 was informally renamed "Zez's Office." Badlander spent most downtime in the command room, smoking something that smelled like burning rubber and old regret, occasionally radioing outposts just to tell them they were doing it wrong. BULLHAWK — Assistant. Bullhawk's exact role aboard the Dugong was never formally established, which suited him perfectly. Officially tasked with general support duties, he was in practice found everywhere he shouldn't be and nowhere he was needed. Deeply affiliated with the Clown Mother faith — though he consistently denied this while wearing the mask and honk — Bullhawk operated in a permanent state of cheerful chaos that the rest of the crew had simply learned to factor in, like flooding or oxygen loss. Cause of death: unknown. This is also suspicious. ZEZ — Electrician. The Dugong's reactor had, under Zez's stewardship, a documented operational reliability of approximately 50%. Zez was confided that this was "industry standard." It was not. He approached electrical systems with creative confidence and a stunning unfamiliarity with the relationship between explosive crates and batteries, a gap in knowledge that proved educational for everyone within blast radius. In his defense, the beeping sound usually meant that eggs are ready. In everyone else's defense, we had no eggs onboard the submarine. Zez was punched by Badlander on fourteen separate logged occasions. The actual number is believed to be higher. REINDEER — Security Officer. Dependable. Professional. The only crew member who could be described as prepared. Reindeer took his responsibilities seriously, his aim seriously, and the distinction between "confirmed hostile" and "moving in an unsettling manner" somewhat loosely. First to respond to any threat. Also first to respond to perceived threats, theoretical threats, and one incident involving a Zez. If it had a swimming bladder, four legs, or a clown mask with honk in hand, Reindeer had already made a decision about it. The Dugong had zero onboard clown incidents. Whether this was policy or fear remains debated. JAGER — Engineer. In a crew of remarkable individuals, Jager was the one keeping the hull together — literally. Not a single breach occurred under his watch that wasn't caused by someone else, which in this crew is a staggering achievement. He was methodical, competent, and quietly furious at all times. His cousin, Cell, briefly joined the crew as a junior engineer and lasted eleven days before a routine procedure performed by Sleep resulted in an involuntary amputation and immediate airlock deployment. Jager never fully discussed this as he really didn't like the cousin himself. The subject was considered closed.

🔒 Mission Objective — Classification: Restricted
The primary objective of this expedition is listed in EuropaCorp internal records as "cargo retrieval — contents classified, authorisation level: Sleepshenok Jr. only." Sleepshenok Jr. has been asked to elaborate on what exactly is being retrieved, from where, and why it requires a fully crewed submarine rather than a standard salvage drone. He has described the mission as "personal." He has also described it as "definitely not related to the blue jars, Zez, and Endworm." These statements have not been reconciled. Crew members are not required to know the full details of the mission objective at time of application. They will be informed at depth. At that point, turning back will not be an option, which Sleepshenok Jr. assures us is "fine."
The Dugong is gone. The crew is gone. Sleep's collection of carefully harvested surplus organs — kidneys, livers, one item no one was able to identify — was, however, recovered by EuropaCorp salvage teams and sold on the secondary market at a modest but respectable profit. Under Europa Deep inheritance law, the proceeds passed to Sleepshenok Jr. — Sleep's long-distance relative, a man who knew him primarily through rumours and a handwritten note that read: "Don't open the blue jars. Also Zez is perfect specimen, further research necessary, carry on my legacy!" Sleepshenok Jr. has elected to spend this inheritance on a second-hand submarine and a fresh crew. The reasons for this decision are unclear. Several people have asked. He has not answered satisfactorily. The vessel in question — currently listed as [NAME PENDING INSURANCE APPROVAL] — has been described by the seller as "pressure-tested to 800 meters and only technically on fire once." We will be operating somewhat deeper than 1000 meters, but should be fine. All positions are open. Compensation is negotiable. Survival is not guaranteed, not implied, and should not be inferred from any part of this document. Previous crew equipment may still be aboard. Finders keepers. PSA: Sleepshenok Jr. is not the captain. He is just a dude with marks to buy a submarine for a one way trip. If you are reading this notice and still considering applying — welcome. You're exactly what we're looking for. — Sleepshenok Jr., Crew Leader, EuropaCorp Licensed Contractor #4471-B "I didn't know my relative personally. But I feel like I knew him well enough."
